About BBSO

Perched above Big Bear Lake, the observatory has been at the forefront of solar physics for over five decades.

A Legacy of Solar Observation

Founded in 1969 by Caltech on a causeway in Big Bear Lake, the observatory takes advantage of stable air and clear skies over the water. The unique lakeside location provides exceptional seeing conditions for solar observations.

In 1997, ownership transferred to the New Jersey Institute of Technology (NJIT), which has operated and expanded the facility since. Today, BBSO is home to the 1.6-meter Goode Solar Telescope, one of the largest and most capable solar telescopes in the world.
