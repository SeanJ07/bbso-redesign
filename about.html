About BBSO

Perched above the waters of Big Bear Lake, the observatory has been at the forefront of solar physics for over five decades.

A Legacy of Solar Observation

The Big Bear Solar Observatory was established in 1969 by the California Institute of Technology on a causeway in the middle of Big Bear Lake, taking advantage of the stable air and clear skies over the water.

In 1997, ownership transferred to the New Jersey Institute of Technology (NJIT), which has since operated and expanded the facility. The observatory's unique lakeside location provides exceptional seeing conditions for solar observations.

Today, BBSO is home to the 1.6-meter Goode Solar Telescope (GST), one of the largest and most capable solar telescopes in the world, producing diffraction-limited images of the solar surface with unprecedented detail.
