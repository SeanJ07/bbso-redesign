Science Nuggets

Raindrops in the Solar Corona

Scientists from the NSF National Solar Observatory and NJIT produced the finest images of the Sun's corona to date using a new coronal adaptive optics system. Results published in Nature Astronomy.

May 2025 — Schmidt, Schad, Yurchyshyn, Gorceix, Rimmele, Goode

Death of a Sunspot

High spatial and temporal resolution of the GST instruments allowed analysis of sunspot decay. Area and total magnetic flux display an exponential decrease, with 22 moving magnetic features identified.

June 2023 — Astron. Astrophys., 686, id.A75

Magnetic Fields above Sunspot Light Bridges

NIRIS magnetograms combined with HMI magnetograms and nonlinear force-free field models revealed a field-free region and different velocity structures inside light bridges.

August 2022 — Astrophys. J. Lett., 935, L27

Are Switchbacks produced by the Chromospheric Network?

High-resolution H-alpha images from the GST show spicules along a chromospheric network present similar scales to the medium scale of Parker Solar Probe switchbacks.

August 2022 — Lee et al., Astrophys. J. Lett., 935, L27

Kilogauss Magnetic Fields on the Sun

NIRIS data revealed thin flux tubes of 200-400 km in diameter with field strength of 1000-2000 G. HMI power spectra exhibited the same spectral index of -1 across spatial scales.

March 2022 — Astrophys. J., 928, 41

Flare Emission in Helium Line 10830

Flare emission/absorption in helium line 10830 at multiple spectral positions observed with the GST.

January 2022

Earth is Reflecting Less Light

Earth is reflecting less light. It's not clear if that's a trend.

January 2022

Turbulent Magnetic Fields in Quiet Sun

Analysis of quiet-sun turbulence on the basis of SDO/HMI and Goode Solar Telescope data.

September 2020 — MNRAS, 497, Issue 4, 5405-5412

Physical Characteristics of Umbral Dots

Physical characteristics of umbral dots derived from high-resolution observations.
