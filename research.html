Research & Data

From solar flares to coronal rain, BBSO researchers study our dynamic Sun using world-class instruments and open data.

Research Programs

BBSO's research spans the breadth of solar physics, from the photosphere to the outer corona.

Intermittency in Solar Convection

Turbulent convection patterns and intermittency in the solar photosphere using high-resolution GST observations.

Solar Oscillations

Helioseismology and magnetohydrodynamic waves propagating through the solar interior and atmosphere.

→

Space Weather

Solar flares, coronal mass ejections, and their impact on Earth's space environment and technology.

→

Full-Disk H-alpha Observations

Continuous full-disk monitoring in H-alpha, capturing flares, filaments, and prominences in real time.

→

Earthshine

Measuring sunlight reflected from Earth to the Moon, providing data on Earth's reflectance and climate monitoring.

The Goode Solar Telescope

The GST is a 1.6-meter clear-aperture off-axis Gregorian system with active and adaptive optics. It resolves features as small as 50 km on the Sun's surface.

Equipped with the Near-Infrared Imaging Spectropolarimeter (NIRIS), the Broadband Filter Imager (BFI), and the Fast Imaging Solar Spectrograph (FISS).
